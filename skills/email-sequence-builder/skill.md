# Email Sequence Builder

Turn one-time visitors into engaged, active users through automated email sequences.

Based on **Chapter 07 - Launch Day** and **Chapter 08 - First Users** from Super Distributor playbooks.

---

## What This Skill Does

Generates complete, ready-to-use email sequences for different user journey stages:
1. **Welcome Series** (3-5 emails) - New signups → First value
2. **Onboarding** (3-4 emails) - New users → Active users
3. **Nurture** (ongoing) - Keep users engaged
4. **Re-engagement** (2-3 emails) - Win back inactive users

---

## Step 1: Choose Your Sequence Type

Which email sequence do you need?

**A. Welcome Series** - For new waitlist signups or first-time visitors
**B. Onboarding** - For users who just signed up or started using your product
**C. Nurture** - For keeping active users engaged
**D. Re-engagement** - For bringing back inactive users
**E. All of the above** - Complete email system

**Which sequence?** (Type A, B, C, D, or E)

---

## Step 2: Provide Product Information

For any sequence, I need basic product context:

**Question 1: Product name**

**Question 2: What does your product do?**
- One sentence, plain language
- Example: "Open-source distribution playbooks for indie makers"

**Question 3: What's the main outcome users get?**
- The result, not the process
- Example: "Go from zero to your first 100 users"

**Question 4: Who is it for?**
- Specific user type
- Example: "Indie makers and solo founders"

**Question 5: What's the primary call-to-action?**
- What action do you want users to take?
- Examples: "Star on GitHub", "Try it now", "Read the playbooks", "Book a demo"

**Question 6: Your name and role**
- How should you sign emails?
- Example: "Grace, Creator of Super Distributor"

## Sequence A: Welcome Series (New Signups)

**For users who:** Just joined your waitlist, subscribed, or showed initial interest

**Goal:** Build trust, set expectations, deliver quick value

### Email Flow (5 emails over 7 days)

#### Email 1: Welcome + Immediate Value (Day 0 - Sent immediately)

**Subject:** `Welcome to [Product Name] 👋`

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for signing up for [Product Name].

You're in. Here's what happens next:

→ [Immediate value - a resource, guide, or quick win]
→ [When to expect updates - "I'll email you when X"]
→ [How to get help - "Reply to this email anytime"]

[1-2 sentence reminder of what they signed up for]

Looking forward to having you here.

[Your name]

P.S. [Optional: Quick tip or resource they can use right now]
```

---

#### Email 2: Core Value Explanation (Day 2)

**Subject:** `Here's how [Product Name] works`

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

Quick update: I want to make sure you know how to get the most out of [Product Name].

Here's the simplest way to think about it:

[3-step explanation of how it works]
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

[Link to getting started guide or main resource]

Most people start with [specific action]. That's usually the fastest way to see results.

Questions? Just reply.

[Your name]
```

---

#### Email 3: Quick Win / Use Case (Day 4)

**Subject:** `[Specific outcome] in [timeframe]`

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

I wanted to share a quick win you can get with [Product Name].

[Describe a specific use case or result]

Example:
[1-2 sentence story of someone using your product successfully]

You can do the same thing:
→ [Step-by-step action they can take]

It takes about [timeframe] and you'll have [outcome].

Try it and let me know how it goes.

[Your name]
```

---

#### Email 4: Community + Resources (Day 6)

**Subject:** `You're not alone`

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

Quick note: You're now part of a community of [number or type] of people using [Product Name] to [outcome].

Here's what's available:

→ [Community link - Discord, Slack, GitHub Discussions]
→ [Documentation or guides]
→ [Social media or blog]

The community is super helpful. Don't hesitate to ask questions.

And if you have feedback or ideas, I'm always listening.

[Your name]
```

---

#### Email 5: First Ask (Day 7)

**Subject:** `Can I ask you something?`

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

You've been with us for a week. First of all, thank you.

I have a quick ask:

[Primary CTA - Star on GitHub / Share with a friend / Try a feature / Give feedback]

→ [Link to action]

Why I'm asking:
[1-2 sentences explaining why this helps]

No pressure. But if you've found value in [Product Name], this would mean a lot.

Thanks for being here.

[Your name]
```

## Sequence B: Onboarding (New Users)

**For users who:** Just signed up, downloaded, or started using your product

**Goal:** Get them to "aha moment", activate core features, become power users

### Email Flow (4 emails over 10 days)

**Subject:** `Let's get you started with [Product Name]`

**Body:**
```

Welcome to [Product Name]! Let's get you set up.

Your first action:

[Link to start]

This should take about [timeframe]. Once you do this, you'll [immediate outcome].

Need help? Reply to this email or [link to docs/support].

Let's go.

[Your name]
```

---

#### Email 2: Feature Spotlight (Day 3)

**Subject:** `Most people miss this`

**Body:**
```

Here's how it works:
[Explain the feature in 2-3 sentences]

Why this matters:
[The outcome or benefit]

Try it:
→ [Link or instructions]

This is one of those features that seems small but makes a huge difference.

[Your name]
```


## Sequence D: Re-engagement (Win Back Inactive Users)

**For users who:** Signed up but never activated, or were active but stopped using

**Goal:** Bring them back, identify why they left, offer a clear path to return

### Email Flow (3 emails over 7 days)

#### Email 1: We Miss You (Day 0)

**Subject:** `We miss you`

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

I noticed you haven't used [Product Name] in a while.

No hard feelings — but I wanted to check in.

→ Is there something that's not working for you?
→ Did you hit a blocker?
→ Can I help with anything?

Reply and let me know. If [Product Name] isn't the right fit, that's okay. But if there's something I can fix, I want to know.

[Your name]
```

---

#### Email 2: What You're Missing (Day 3)

**Subject:** `Here's what's new since you left`

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

In case you haven't been following along, here's what's new in [Product Name]:

→ [Update 1]
→ [Update 2]
→ [Update 3]

Things have changed a lot. If the reason you stopped using it was [common pain point], that's been fixed.

Want to give it another try?
→ [Link to product]

[Your name]
```

---

#### Email 3: Last Call (Day 7)

**Subject:** `One last thing before I go`

**Body:**
```
Hi [Name],

This is my last email (unless you want to hear from me).

If [Product Name] isn't for you, no problem. I'll stop emailing.

But before I do, I have one question:

→ What would it take for you to use [Product Name] again?

Reply and let me know. Even if the answer is "nothing", I want to understand.

Thanks for giving it a shot.

[Your name]

P.S. If you don't want to hear from me anymore, [unsubscribe link].
```

## Email Best Practices

### Subject Lines
- ✅ Short (5-7 words)
- ✅ Specific, not vague
- ✅ Personal, not salesy
- ❌ Avoid "Re:" or "Fwd:" tricks
- ❌ No ALL CAPS or excessive emojis

### Body Copy
- ✅ One idea per email
- ✅ Conversational tone
- ✅ Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- ✅ Clear CTA (one primary action)
- ❌ No walls of text
- ❌ No multiple CTAs (confusing)

### Timing
- ✅ Send during work hours (9 AM - 5 PM their timezone)
- ✅ Avoid weekends for onboarding emails
- ✅ Space emails at least 2 days apart
- ❌ Don't email daily (nurture sequences)

### Deliverability
- ✅ Use a real email address (not noreply@)
- ✅ Authenticate your domain (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
- ✅ Make unsubscribe easy
- ❌ Don't buy email lists
- ❌ Don't send to people who didn't opt-in

## What Comes Next

After I generate your sequences:

1. **Set up your email tool** (ConvertKit, Buttondown, etc.)
2. **Import the sequences** (copy-paste or upload)
3. **Test with yourself** (send test emails, check formatting)
4. **Launch** (activate automation)
5. **Monitor metrics:**
   - Open rate (20-30% is good)
   - Click rate (3-5% is good)
   - Reply rate (1-2% is great)
   - Unsubscribe rate (<0.5% is normal)

6. **Iterate based on data:**
   - Low opens → Test subject lines
   - Low clicks → Clarify CTA
   - High unsubscribes → Reduce frequency or improve targeting
