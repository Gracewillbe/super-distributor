## Competitor Analyzer

**Claude Code skill for Super Distributor**

Systematically analyze your competitive landscape and find your unique positioning angle.

---

## What It Does

This skill helps you map competitors and identify the gap you can own.

**You provide:**
- Your product description and target user
- List of competitors and alternatives
- Information about each competitor's positioning

**You get:**
- Complete competitive matrix
- Gap analysis (white space, weak positions, abandoned positions)
- Positioning recommendations
- Validated positioning statement
- One-sentence positioning line

---

## How to Use

### In Claude Code

```
Use the competitor-analyzer skill
```

The skill will guide you through:
1. Define your product and problem
2. List competitors (direct, indirect, DIY)
3. Analyze what positions are taken
4. Identify gaps in the market
5. Craft your positioning statement
6. Validate with testing checklist

---

## What You'll Get

### 1. Competitive Matrix

A systematic analysis of all alternatives:

| Alternative | Position | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-------------|----------|-----------|------------|
| Direct competitors | What users think of them | What they do well | Where they fail |
| Indirect substitutes | Alternative approaches | Why users choose them | Gaps they leave |
| DIY methods | Manual solutions | Benefits | Pain points |

---

### 2. Gap Analysis

Three types of positioning opportunities:

**White Space** - Positions no one owns
- Underserved segments
- Overlooked use cases
- Needs without solutions

**Weak Positions** - Claimed but not delivered
- Promises competitors make but don't keep
- Areas where you can do it better

**Abandoned Positions** - Used to exist, now neglected
- Solutions that went away
- Positions competitors moved away from

---

### 3. Positioning Statement

A complete positioning framework:

```
For [target user],
who [has this specific problem],
[Your product] is [category]
that [unique differentiator].

Unlike [main alternative],
[Your product] [key difference].
```

Plus a one-sentence positioning line users will remember.

---

### 4. Validation Checklist

Tests to confirm your positioning works:
- 5-second test (can strangers understand it?)
- "Why you?" test (does it explain differentiation?)
- Memory test (is it sticky?)
- Differentiation test (is it unique?)

## Tips for Best Results

### Finding Competitors
1. **Google "[your problem] solutions"** - See what shows up
2. **Check Product Hunt / Hacker News** - Find similar products
3. **Ask in communities** - Reddit, X, Discord - "How do you solve [problem]?"
4. **Look at alternatives** - What do users do without your product?

### Analyzing Positioning
1. **Don't use competitor marketing copy** - Look at what users say about them
2. **Check reviews and complaints** - Where do competitors fail?
3. **Find the pattern** - What do all competitors have in common?
4. **Look for the gap** - What's missing?

### Crafting Your Position
1. **Be specific, not broad** - "For indie makers" > "For anyone who builds"
2. **Own one thing** - Not "best at everything"
3. **Make it defensible** - Can you actually deliver on this?
4. **Test with real users** - Don't just assume it works

---

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Listing too many competitors** - Focus on top 3-5, not every product ever
❌ **Only looking at direct competitors** - Miss the DIY and substitute solutions
❌ **Copying competitor positioning** - "Like X, but better" makes you #2
❌ **Being for everyone** - Narrow = sharp, broad = forgettable
❌ **Inventing new categories** - Position within categories users understand
❌ **Not validating** - Test your positioning before committing to it

## After You Complete This

### Immediate Next Steps
1. **Run the validation tests** - 5-second test with 3 people minimum
2. **Document your positioning** - Save the output for reference
3. **Use messaging-generator skill** - Turn positioning into words

### Execution
- **Align product** - Cut features that don't support positioning
- **Align marketing** - Rewrite landing page and copy
- **Align community** - Engage where your users are

### Long-term
- **Commit to your position** - Don't waffle or try to be everything
- **Reinforce consistently** - Every touchpoint should reinforce it
- **Evolve deliberately** - Only change positioning when you've saturated your niche

## Positioning Philosophy

**From Chapter 03:**

> "Positioning is not what you do to a product. It's what you do to the mind of the prospect."

Good positioning is:
- **Simple** - One clear concept
- **Memorable** - Repeatable in one sentence
- **Different** - Owns a unique position
- **Defensible** - You can actually deliver on it
- **Narrow** - Specific beats generic

Bad positioning:
- Tries to be everything to everyone
- Lists features instead of owning a concept
- Copies what competitors already own
- Too clever or complex to remember
