## Build-in-Public Story Arcs

Content isn't random. It follows **narrative arcs** over time.

### Arc 1: Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-4)
- Introduce yourself and problem
- Share build progress
- Ask for feedback
- Build anticipation

### Arc 2: Launch (Weeks 5-6)
- Announce launch
- Share results (users, feedback)
- Iterate publicly
- Thank supporters

### Arc 3: Post-Launch (Weeks 7-12)
- Share user stories
- Teach what you learned
- Introduce new features
- Build community

**Your content will follow these arcs automatically based on your timeline.**

## Platform-Specific Adaptations

If you're posting to multiple platforms, here's how to adapt:

**Twitter/X:**
- Short, punchy
- Use threads for depth
- Engage in replies
- Post 1-2x daily

**LinkedIn:**
- More professional tone
- Longer posts (1000+ chars)
- Focus on lessons and insights
- Post 2-3x weekly

**Reddit:**
- Share in relevant subreddits
- Lead with value, not promotion
- Be a community member first
- Post 1-2x weekly

**Blog/Newsletter:**
- Weekly roundup of insights
- Deep dives on topics
- Behind-the-scenes stories
- Weekly or bi-weekly

## Execution Tips

### Before You Start
- [ ] Set up your platform profiles (bio, links)
- [ ] Prepare 2-3 weeks of content in advance
- [ ] Choose a scheduling tool (Buffer, Typefully, Hypefury)
- [ ] Block time for engagement (30 min daily)

### While Posting
- [ ] Don't just post and ghost — engage with replies
- [ ] Share others' content (not just your own)
- [ ] Reply to comments within the first hour
- [ ] Track what performs well (save top posts)

### After 30 Days
- [ ] Review analytics (impressions, engagement, clicks)
- [ ] Double down on what works
- [ ] Cut what doesn't resonate
- [ ] Update calendar for next month

## Avoiding Burnout

Content calendars fail when they become a chore. Here's how to stay sustainable:

### The 80/20 Rule
- 80% of results come from 20% of posts
- Focus on quality topics, not quantity
- It's okay to skip days

### Batch Content Creation
- Write 5-10 posts in one sitting
- Schedule them in advance
- Frees up mental energy

### Repurpose Everything
- Turn threads into blog posts
- Turn blog posts into threads
- Turn comments into new posts
- Recycle evergreen content

### Take Breaks
- It's okay to go quiet for a week
- You don't need to post daily forever
- Consistency > perfection
