# Messaging Generator

This skill helps you translate your product positioning into words that stick.

Based on **Chapter 04 - Messaging** from Super Distributor playbooks.

---

## What This Skill Does

Takes your positioning work and generates:
1. **3 One-Liner Variations** (10-second pitch in different formats)
2. **Value Proposition** (30-second version with problem + differentiation)
3. **3-5 Core Message Pillars** (2-minute messaging architecture)

---

## Step 1: Gather Positioning Inputs

I'll ask you a series of questions to understand your positioning. Answer as specifically as you can.

**Question 1: What problem does your product solve?**
- What pain point do your users already know they have?
- What's the "I built something but..." problem?

**Question 2: What is your product?**
- In one sentence, what does it do?
- Don't overthink this - just describe it plainly.

**Question 3: Who is it for?**
- Be specific. Not "everyone" or "people who need X"
- E.g., "indie makers", "fast-moving product teams", "solo founders"

**Question 4: What makes you different?**
- Why would someone choose you over competitors or alternatives?
- What's your unique approach or positioning?

**Question 5: What are you NOT?**
- What category or approach are you explicitly avoiding?
- E.g., "Not enterprise marketing advice", "Not a complex tool"

**Question 6: What's the outcome users want?**
- After using your product, what can they do or achieve?
- Focus on the outcome, not the features.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

As you use this messaging, watch out for:

❌ **Feature Soup** - Lead with the outcome, not the features
❌ **Jargon Overload** - If a 12-year-old can't understand it, rewrite it
❌ **Being For Everyone** - Pick one user. Everyone else is a bonus.
❌ **Burying the Lede** - Start with what they care about, not what you're proud of
❌ **No Differentiation** - Say what you're NOT, or how you're different

---

## Final Litmus Test

Before you ship your messaging, run it through these 5 questions:

| Question | Your Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| **Is it clear?** | Can a stranger repeat it back to you? |
| **Is it simple?** | One breath or less? |
| **Is it specific?** | Does it say who it's for (and who it's not)? |
| **Is it different?** | Could a competitor say the same thing? (If yes, rewrite.) |
| **Is it compelling?** | Does it make someone want to know more? |

If you fail any of these, go back to Step 2.
