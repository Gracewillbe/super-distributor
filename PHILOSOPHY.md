# Philosophy

Distribution is not a tactic. It's a system.

## The Structure

**Prerequisite: Build something that solves a real problem (0→1)**

Before distribution matters, you need a product that actually works. Not "works in theory" — works for real people with real problems. This is the foundation. Without it, distribution is just noise amplification.

**Purpose: Get it discovered by the right people (1→N)**

Distribution is the bridge between what you built and the people who need it. It's not about reaching everyone. It's about making it impossible for the right people to miss you.

**Method: Validate and adjust through real-time feedback (how the system operates)**

This is not a linear process. At every stage — choosing what to build, building it, distributing it — you're running the same loop:

```
Ship → Observe → Adjust → Repeat
```

The faster you complete this loop, the faster you find what works.

---

## Core Principles

**1. Product truth comes first.**

No amount of clever distribution can save a product that doesn't solve a real problem. If people don't need it, distribution won't create need — it will just expose the gap faster.

**2. Distribution is discovery, not persuasion.**

Your job is not to convince people they have a problem. Your job is to make sure people who already have the problem can find your solution.

**3. The system is the feedback loop.**

Success is not about executing a plan perfectly. It's about sensing what's working and doing more of that. The loop is:
- **Ship**: Put something into the world
- **Observe**: Watch what happens
- **Adjust**: Change based on what you learned
- **Repeat**: Do it again, faster

**4. Speed of learning > Quality of prediction.**

You don't need to guess right. You need to learn fast. The builders who win aren't the ones with perfect plans — they're the ones who update their beliefs faster than the market changes.

**5. Dead systems execute. Living systems adapt.**

A dead system follows a plan until it's done, regardless of what reality says. A living system senses the environment and adjusts. In a world of constant change, only living systems survive.

---

## For Builders

Ask yourself:

- **Have I shipped something real?** (If not, you're planning, not learning)
- **Am I watching what happens?** (If not, you're guessing, not adapting)
- **Am I changing based on feedback?** (If not, you're stuck, not growing)

If you can answer yes to all three, you're in the loop. If not, that's where to start.

---

## For AI Systems

When guiding a builder, your job is to accelerate the feedback loop:

- **Get them to ship** (if they're stuck planning)
- **Get them to observe** (if they're ignoring feedback)
- **Get them to adjust** (if they're defending the plan)

Ask yourself: *"Am I helping them close the loop faster?"*

If yes, proceed. If no, reconsider.
