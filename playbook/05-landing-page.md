# Landing Page: Ship a Page That Converts

> "A landing page is not a website. It's a single-purpose sales tool with one job: turn visitors into users."

## The Landing Page Paradox

You have positioning. You have messaging. You're ready to launch.

But here's what happens to most indie makers:

- Spend 3 months building the product
- Spend 2 weeks obsessing over the landing page design
- Launch with something generic that converts at 0.5%
- Wonder why nobody signs up

**The truth: Your landing page isn't a design project. It's a conversion system.**

And most indie makers treat it like an art gallery instead of a sales machine.

**If your landing page doesn't convert visitors into users, it doesn't matter how beautiful it is.**

---

## What a Landing Page Actually Is

A landing page is not:
- ❌ Your full website (that comes later, if ever)
- ❌ A portfolio of features (nobody cares)
- ❌ A design showcase (you're not Dribbble)

**A landing page is a focused, single-purpose page with one goal: get someone to take action.**

For indie makers, that action is usually:
- Sign up for early access
- Download the tool
- Book a demo
- Join the waitlist
- Star the repo

**Good landing pages:**
- Make the value clear in 5 seconds
- Remove all friction from the CTA
- Speak to one specific person
- Look professional without over-designing

**Bad landing pages:**
- Bury the value in jargon
- Force visitors to scroll forever
- Try to be for everyone
- Use stock photos and generic copy

---

## Why Most Landing Pages Fail

Let's be honest about why your first landing page will probably suck:

### Mistake 1: Starting With Design
❌ **Wrong order:** Pick a template → Fill in placeholder text → Wonder why it doesn't convert

✅ **Right order:** Write copy → Structure the message → Add design last

**Copy comes first. Design amplifies good copy. It can't fix bad copy.**

---

### Mistake 2: Explaining Features, Not Outcomes
❌ **Bad:** "Our platform features AI-powered analytics, real-time collaboration, and custom integrations."

✅ **Good:** "Turn messy data into clear decisions — in seconds, not hours."

**Nobody buys features. They buy the outcome.**

---

### Mistake 3: Talking to Everyone
❌ **Bad:** "Perfect for startups, enterprises, freelancers, agencies, students, and hobbyists."

✅ **Good:** "Built for product teams shipping fast."

**When you talk to everyone, you convince no one.**

---

### Mistake 4: Weak or Hidden CTA
❌ **Bad:** Scroll three screens to find a "Learn More" button

✅ **Good:** Bold CTA above the fold: "Start Free Trial"

**If they have to hunt for the CTA, you've already lost.**

---

### Mistake 5: Over-Designing
❌ **Bad:** Custom animations, parallax scrolling, video backgrounds, infinite scroll

✅ **Good:** Clean typography, fast load time, one clear path

**Indie makers over-design because they're afraid of shipping something simple. Simple converts.**

## The 5-Section Landing Page Framework

Here's a proven structure that works for 90% of indie products. You can ship this in a weekend.

### Section 1: Hero (5-Second Test)
**Job:** Make the value crystal clear in 5 seconds.

**Components:**
- **Headline** — One sentence. What you do and who it's for.
- **Subheadline** — One sentence. How you do it or why it matters.
- **CTA Button** — One action. Make it specific.
- **Visual** (optional) — Screenshot, diagram, or illustration. Not a stock photo.

**Example (Super Distributor):**

```
Headline: Ship from zero to your first users
Subheadline: Open-source playbooks, tools, and templates for indie makers who can't afford a marketing team.
CTA: Browse Playbooks →
Visual: Screenshot of the playbook directory
```

**Hero Copywriting Rules:**
- Use "you" language, not "we" language
- Lead with the outcome, not the process
- Make the CTA verb-specific ("Browse Playbooks" > "Get Started")
- Test: If someone only reads this section, do they know what you do?

---

### Section 2: Problem (Why This Exists)
**Job:** Validate that you understand their pain.

**Components:**
- **Problem statement** — 2-3 sentences describing the pain
- **Current alternatives** (optional) — Why existing solutions fail
- **Emotional hook** — Make them feel seen

**Example:**

```
Most indie makers are great at building — but terrible at distribution.

You spend months on a product, then launch into silence. No users. No feedback. No idea what to do next.

Marketing guides are written for companies with teams and budgets. Not solo founders starting from zero.
```

**Problem Section Rules:**
- Be specific. Generic pain = no emotional connection.
- Use their words. Pull from Reddit, Twitter, customer interviews.
- Don't make it too long. This isn't therapy.

---

### Section 3: Solution (How It Works)
**Job:** Show how you solve the problem.

**Components:**
- **Solution statement** — 1-2 sentences on your approach
- **Key benefits or features** — 3-5 bullet points (outcomes, not features)
- **Social proof** (optional) — Testimonial, stat, or logos

**Example:**

```
Super Distributor gives you the playbooks and tools to launch, market, and grow — without a marketing team.

✓ Step-by-step playbooks from positioning to first users
✓ Ready-to-use tools (Twitter automation, Grok prompts, GitHub templates)
✓ Battle-tested by real launches, not theory
✓ Free and open-source. No signup required.
```

**Solution Section Rules:**
- Use bullets, not paragraphs. Easier to scan.
- Lead with outcomes ("Get your first users") not features ("Includes 9 playbooks").
- Show proof if you have it. No proof? Skip it. Don't fake it.

---

### Section 4: How It Works (Optional, Use If Complex)
**Job:** Reduce confusion. Show the process.

**Components:**
- **3-step process** — Keep it simple
- **Visual diagram** (optional) — Arrows, numbers, screenshots

**Example:**

```
1. Pick your stage — Market research, positioning, launch, or growth
2. Follow the playbook — Step-by-step guides with real examples
3. Use the tools — Templates, scripts, and automation ready to go
```

**How It Works Rules:**
- Only include this if your product isn't immediately obvious
- 3 steps max. More = confusing.
- Use action verbs ("Pick", "Follow", "Use")

---

### Section 5: CTA (Drive the Action)
**Job:** Remove all friction. Make the next step crystal clear.

**Components:**
- **CTA Button** — Same as hero, or a variation
- **Reassurance** — Remove objections ("Free", "No credit card", "5-min setup")
- **Secondary CTA** (optional) — Lower-commitment option (e.g., "Star on GitHub")

**Example:**

```
[Primary CTA Button: Browse Playbooks]

Free and open-source. No signup required.

[Secondary CTA: Star on GitHub →]
```

**CTA Section Rules:**
- Make the button contrast. Don't hide it.
- Remove friction: "No credit card" > "Free trial"
- Offer a secondary CTA if the primary is high-commitment (e.g., "Book a demo" vs "Read docs")

---

## Advanced: The 7-Section Version (If You Need More Proof)

If your product is complex, expensive, or requires trust, add these sections **between Solution and CTA**:

### Section 6: Features or Benefits Deep Dive
**Job:** Give details for people who need more convincing.

**Format:** 3-6 feature cards with icon + headline + description.

---

### Section 7: Social Proof
**Job:** Build trust through testimonials, case studies, or usage stats.

**Examples:**
- "Trusted by 10,000+ indie makers"
- "Featured on Product Hunt, Hacker News, and Indie Hackers"
- Testimonials with real names and photos (fake = obvious)

**Rule:** If you don't have real proof yet, skip it. Don't fabricate.

---

### Section 8: FAQ (Objection Handling)
**Job:** Answer the top 3-5 questions that stop people from converting.

**Examples:**
- "Is this really free?" → Yes, open-source MIT license.
- "Do I need marketing experience?" → No, playbooks assume zero experience.
- "How is this different from [competitor]?" → We're built for indie makers, not enterprises.

## Landing Page Copywriting Principles

### Principle 1: Clarity Over Cleverness
❌ **Bad:** "Revolutionizing the future of work"
✅ **Good:** "Project management for fast-moving teams"

**Nobody has time to decode your clever headline.**

---

### Principle 2: Outcomes Over Features
❌ **Bad:** "Includes 9 playbooks and 12 tools"
✅ **Good:** "Go from zero to your first 100 users"

**Sell the destination, not the map.**

---

### Principle 3: Specificity Over Vagueness
❌ **Bad:** "Boost your productivity"
✅ **Good:** "Cut meeting time from 10 hours to 2 hours per week"

**Specific outcomes are believable. Vague promises are not.**

---

### Principle 4: "You" Language Over "We" Language
❌ **Bad:** "We built a platform that helps teams collaborate"
✅ **Good:** "Ship faster without endless meetings"

**Make it about them, not you.**

---

### Principle 5: Active Voice Over Passive Voice
❌ **Bad:** "Users can be empowered to achieve their goals"
✅ **Good:** "Reach your goals faster"

**Active = energy. Passive = boring.**

## Design Principles for Non-Designers

You don't need to be a designer to ship a landing page that converts. Follow these rules:

### Rule 1: Use a Proven Template
Don't design from scratch. Use:
- **Carrd** (easiest, $19/year)
- **Webflow** (more control, steeper learning curve)
- **Framer** (design-forward, good for polished look)
- **HTML + Tailwind** (if you code)

**Pick one. Don't template-hop.**

---

### Rule 2: Limit Your Colors
- **1 primary color** (for CTA buttons and accents)
- **1-2 neutral colors** (black, gray, white)
- **No gradients, no rainbows**

**More colors = more distraction.**

---

### Rule 3: Use Good Typography
- **2 fonts max** (one for headlines, one for body)
- **Use system fonts if unsure** (Inter, SF Pro, Helvetica)
- **Big headlines** (48px+), **readable body text** (18px+)

**Good typography = 80% of good design.**

---

### Rule 4: Add White Space
- Don't cram content together
- Let sections breathe
- Wide margins, generous padding

**White space = luxury and clarity.**

---

### Rule 5: Use Real Visuals (Not Stock Photos)
- **Screenshots** of your product (even if ugly, it's real)
- **Simple diagrams** (use Excalidraw, Figma, or Whimsical)
- **No stock photos of people pointing at laptops**

**Real > polished.**

## The Weekend Landing Page Sprint

Here's how to ship a landing page in 2 days:

### Saturday Morning (2 hours): Write the Copy
1. Draft hero headline + subheadline
2. Write problem section (2-3 sentences)
3. Write solution section (1 statement + 3-5 bullets)
4. Write CTA copy
5. **Don't design yet. Just write.**

---

### Saturday Afternoon (3 hours): Structure the Page
1. Pick a template (Carrd, Webflow, Framer, or code)
2. Drop in your copy
3. Add a screenshot or simple visual
4. Style the CTA button (big, bold, contrasting color)
5. **Don't obsess over fonts. Just ship.**

---

### Sunday Morning (2 hours): Polish and Test
1. Read it out loud. Does it sound human?
2. Test the CTA. Does it work? Is it clear?
3. Check mobile. Does it load fast?
4. Send to 3 friends. Do they understand what you do in 5 seconds?
5. **If yes, ship. If no, fix and ship.**

---

### Sunday Afternoon (1 hour): Launch
1. Deploy to a domain (Vercel, Netlify, or your template host)
2. Add analytics (Plausible, Fathom, or Google Analytics)
3. Share on Twitter, Product Hunt, or Indie Hackers
4. **Done. Now iterate based on data.**

## Common Questions

### "Should I build a full website or just a landing page?"
**Landing page first.** Add more pages (About, Blog, Pricing) later if needed. Most indie products don't need a full website at launch.

---

### "What if I don't have screenshots yet?"
Use:
- A mockup (Figma or Excalidraw)
- A diagram of how it works
- A code snippet (if dev tool)
- Nothing (just copy)

**Don't wait for perfect visuals. Ship with what you have.**

---

### "How do I know if my landing page is good?"
Run the **5-Second Test:**
1. Show the page to someone who's never seen it
2. Give them 5 seconds
3. Take it away
4. Ask: "What does this product do? Who is it for?"

**If they can't answer, rewrite your hero.**

---

### "What conversion rate should I expect?"
- **Great:** 10%+ (waitlist or free tool)
- **Good:** 3-5% (sign-up or trial)
- **Average:** 1-2% (paid product, cold traffic)
- **Bad:** <1%

**If you're under 1%, fix your copy or your targeting.**
