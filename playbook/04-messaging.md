# Messaging: Turn Positioning Into Words That Stick

> "Nobody reads ads. People read what interests them. Sometimes it's an ad." — Howard Gossage

## The Messaging Gap

You nailed your positioning. You know what you are, who you're for, and what makes you different.

But when someone asks "So what does it do?", you freeze.

Or you give them a paragraph. Or you list features. Or you say something generic like "We help people work better."

**Here's the problem: Positioning lives in your head. Messaging is what lives in theirs.**

Positioning is strategy. Messaging is execution. And most indie makers skip the translation layer between the two.

**If your positioning doesn't become words people repeat, it doesn't exist.**

---

## What Messaging Actually Is

Messaging is not:
- ❌ Your tagline (that's just one output)
- ❌ Marketing copy (that comes later)
- ❌ Feature descriptions (nobody cares about your features)

**Messaging is the architecture of how you talk about your product.**

It's the system that ensures every time you explain what you built — in a tweet, on a landing page, in a DM, at a conference — you say the same thing, in different ways.

**Good messaging is:**
- **Clear** — A 12-year-old could repeat it
- **Consistent** — Same core idea, different surfaces
- **Compelling** — Makes people want to know more
- **Concise** — Fits in one breath

## The Three Layers of Messaging

Your messaging needs to work at three levels:

### Layer 1: The One-Liner (10 seconds)
**Use case:** Someone asks "What is it?" in an elevator, DM, or tweet.

**Format:** `[What] for [Who]` or `[What] without [Pain]`

**Examples:**
- **Stripe:** Payments for developers
- **Notion:** Docs without the chaos
- **Super Distributor:** The growth harness for indie makers

**Rule:** If they forget it 10 seconds later, it's not good enough.

---

### Layer 2: The Value Prop (30 seconds)
**Use case:** Someone is mildly interested. They want to know "Why should I care?"

**Format:** `[One-liner]. [What it solves]. [How you're different].`

**Example (Super Distributor):**
> "The growth harness for indie makers. Get from zero to your first users — without a marketing team or budget. Open-source playbooks, tools, and templates you can use today."

**Rule:** After hearing this, they should know if it's for them or not.

---

### Layer 3: The Core Message Set (2 minutes)
**Use case:** Landing page, pitch deck, demo call.

**Format:** 3-5 **message pillars** that support your value prop.

Each pillar answers a key objection or highlights a core benefit.

**Example (Super Distributor):**

| Pillar | Message |
|--------|---------|
| **Problem** | Most indie makers are great builders, terrible marketers |
| **Solution** | Open-source playbooks from zero to first users |
| **Proof** | Battle-tested by real launches, not theory |
| **Access** | Free, open-source, ready to use today |
| **Movement** | Distribution is a skill, not magic — and you can learn it |

**Rule:** Every piece of content you make should reinforce at least one pillar.

## The Messaging Framework: Positioning → Message

Here's how to translate your positioning work into messaging.

### Step 1: Extract Your Positioning Inputs

From Chapter 3, you should have:
- ✅ **Problem users already know** (e.g., "I built something but have no users")
- ✅ **Your unique position** (e.g., "Open-source growth tools for indie makers")
- ✅ **What you're NOT** (e.g., "Not enterprise marketing advice")

If you don't have these, go back to Chapter 3.

---

### Step 2: Write Your One-Liner

Use one of these proven formats:

**Format A: [What] for [Who]**
- Stripe = Payments for developers
- Figma = Design tool for teams
- Linear = Issue tracker for fast-moving teams

**Format B: [What] without [Pain]**
- Notion = Docs without the chaos
- Vercel = Deploy without the hassle
- Raycast = Launcher without the bloat

**Format C: The [Category] that [Unique Approach]**
- Basecamp = The project management tool that doesn't make you manage
- Hey = The email service that respects your attention

**Test it:**
- Can you say it in one breath?
- Would someone remember it 10 seconds later?
- Does it make them ask "Tell me more"?

---

### Step 3: Build Your Value Prop

Take your one-liner and expand it with **one problem** and **one differentiator**.

**Template:**
```
[One-liner]. [The problem you solve]. [How you solve it differently].
```

**Example 1: Super Distributor**
> "The growth harness for indie makers. Most builders are stuck at zero users with no marketing budget. We give you open-source playbooks, tools, and templates to launch and grow."

**Example 2: Notion**
> "Docs without the chaos. Teams waste hours searching scattered files. Notion brings everything into one workspace that actually makes sense."

**Rule:** If you can't say it in 3 sentences, it's not tight enough.

---

### Step 4: Define Your Core Message Pillars

Pick **3-5 pillars** that support your value prop. Each pillar addresses a question users have:

| Question | Pillar Type | Example (Super Distributor) |
|----------|-------------|------------------------------|
| Why does this exist? | **Problem** | Indie makers waste months on products nobody finds |
| What does it do? | **Solution** | Open-source playbooks from idea to first users |
| Why should I trust you? | **Proof** | Battle-tested by real launches, not theory |
| What's the catch? | **Access/Pricing** | Free, open-source, no signup required |
| What's the bigger idea? | **Movement/Vision** | Distribution is a skill anyone can learn |

**How to use pillars:**
- Landing page hero = Pillar 1 (Problem or Solution)
- Feature section = Pillar 2-3 (Solution + Proof)
- CTA section = Pillar 4 (Access)
- Footer or About = Pillar 5 (Vision)

---

### Step 5: Adapt Your Message to Different Surfaces

Your core message stays the same. How you say it changes.

| Surface | Length | Format | Example |
|---------|--------|--------|---------|
| **Twitter Bio** | 10 words | One-liner | "Open-source growth tools for indie makers" |
| **Tweet** | 1-2 sentences | One-liner + hook | "You built something. Now what? Super Distributor gives indie makers open-source playbooks to go from 0 → first users." |
| **Landing Page Hero** | 1 headline + 1 subhead | Value prop | **H1:** "Ship from zero to your first users" / **H2:** "Open-source playbooks, tools, and templates for indie makers who can't afford a marketing team." |
| **Product Hunt** | 1 tagline + 3 sentences | One-liner + value prop | **Tagline:** "Growth tools for indie makers" / **Description:** "Most indie builders are great at building, terrible at distribution. Super Distributor is an open-source collection of playbooks and tools to help you launch, market, and land your first users — no budget required." |
| **Pitch (verbal)** | 30 seconds | Full message arc | "I'm working on Super Distributor. It's an open-source growth harness for indie makers. Most builders spend months on a product, then have no idea how to get users. We give them step-by-step playbooks and ready-to-use tools — positioning, landing pages, launch strategy, first 100 users. All free and open-source. Think of it as the missing manual for distribution." |

**Rule:** Same message. Different packaging.

## Common Messaging Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

### Mistake 1: Feature Soup
❌ **Bad:** "We have AI-powered analytics, real-time collaboration, custom workflows, and integrations."

✅ **Good:** "Turn chaos into clarity — one workspace for everything your team needs."

**Fix:** Lead with the outcome, not the features.

---

### Mistake 2: Jargon Overload
❌ **Bad:** "Leverage our robust, scalable infrastructure to optimize cross-functional synergy."

✅ **Good:** "Fast, reliable tools that help teams work together."

**Fix:** If a 12-year-old can't understand it, rewrite it.

---

### Mistake 3: Being For Everyone
❌ **Bad:** "Perfect for startups, enterprises, freelancers, agencies, and students."

✅ **Good:** "Built for fast-moving product teams."

**Fix:** Pick one user. Everyone else is a bonus.

---

### Mistake 4: Burying the Lede
❌ **Bad:** "Founded in 2023, our team of experienced engineers built a platform using cutting-edge technology to help people..."

✅ **Good:** "Get your first users in 7 days. Here's how."

**Fix:** Start with what they care about, not what you're proud of.

---

### Mistake 5: No Differentiation
❌ **Bad:** "The best project management tool for teams."

✅ **Good:** "Project management for people who hate project management." (Basecamp)

**Fix:** Say what you're NOT, or how you're different.

## The Messaging Litmus Test

Before you ship your messaging, run it through these 5 questions:

| Question | Test |
|----------|------|
| **Is it clear?** | Can a stranger repeat it back to you? |
| **Is it simple?** | One breath or less? |
| **Is it specific?** | Does it say who it's for (and who it's not)? |
| **Is it different?** | Could a competitor say the same thing? (If yes, rewrite.) |
| **Is it compelling?** | Does it make someone want to know more? |

If you fail any of these, go back to Step 2.

## TL;DR

- **Positioning = what to say. Messaging = how to say it.**
- Build messaging in 3 layers: One-liner (10s) → Value prop (30s) → Core pillars (2min)
- Adapt the same message to different surfaces (tweet, landing page, pitch)
- Avoid jargon, feature soup, and being for everyone
- Test: Is it clear, simple, specific, different, and compelling?

**Your one-liner is the most important sentence you'll write. Get it right, and everything else gets easier.**
